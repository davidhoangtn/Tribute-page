Mr. Oskar Schindler

"He who saves one life, saves the world entire."

A Nazi, businessman, and war profiteer that later became a war hero who saved 1100 Jewish lives.

Early Life
  • Born in Austria-Hungary on 28 April 1908
  • Worked for his father during 1920s. After his marriage, Oskar left his father's business to work as a sales manager for a Moravian electric company
  • In 1935, Schindler joined the pro-Nazi Sudeten German Party to profit from Hitler's rise to power.

In Poland
  • In 1939, Hitler invaded Poland. Schindler quickly created friendships with key officers in both the German army and the SS (special Nazi unit) by offering black-market goods that were hard to find.
  • At the same time, Schindler hired Irzhak Stern, a Jewish accountant, as the bookkeeper for his enamel factory. Stern convinced Mr.Schindler to hire 56,000 Jews as they were a good source of cheap and reliable labor

His work begins
  • In 1942, Nazis began relocating Krakow's Jews to labor camps, including Schindler's workers. Schindler raced to the train station, and rescued the workers by making a couple of threats and dropping names of his Nazi friends.
  • Early 1943, Nazis liquidated Krakow ghetto: those who could work were sent to Plaszow while the rest were killed or sent to death camps. Schindler established a labor mini-camp within Plaszow to continue employing his workers, after bribing the young SS officer in charge Amon Goeth.

The List
  • Early 1944, all of Plaszow's prisoners were ordered to transport to death camps such as Auschwitz, and then the main camp was forced to be closed including Schindler's factory. Schindler approached Goeth about moving his factory and workers to Czech to continue supplying Hitler's army war supplies.
  • Goeth agreed and tole Schindler to drew up a list, in which is literally a matter of life and death. Schindler came up of a list containing 1100 hundred names including his workers and number of others.
  • Fall of 1944, Schindler began moving his factory to Brunnlitz after a few necessary bribes. 800 men were shipped out in boxcar bounds to Brunnlitz, while 300 women and children were mistakenly sent to Auschwitz. Schindler immediately rescued these women and children, and sent them to Brunnlitz.

End of War
  • 1945, German surrendered. Schindler gathered all his workers to deliver the good news and call for a moment of silence in memory of those who had passed. He also encouraged members of SS who were present to go home with their family peacefully without further bloodshed.
  • Before fleeing with his wife to the US for fear of capture, Schindler's workers endowed him a ring forged out of silver denture, wrote: "He who saves one life, saves the world entire."

Legacy
  • After the war, Schindler were virtually destitute after spending his entire fortunes on bribes and black market supplies for his workers
  • He went on a series of business ventures in cement, raising chicken but none of which were successful. He declared bankruptcy in 1963.
  • In his final days, he survived on the donations sent by Schindlerjuden (Schindler's Jews) from all over the world.
